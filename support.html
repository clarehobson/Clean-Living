Common questions

How do I start or manage my Recovery Pro subscription?

In the app, open the menu and go to your profile, then the Subscription section. New subscribers start with a 14-day free trial. You can manage or cancel any time in your Apple ID settings: Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are handled by Apple. Go to Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions, tap Clean Living, and choose Cancel. If you cancel during the free trial you won't be charged.

How do I restore a purchase?

On the subscription screen, tap Restore Purchases. This re-links a subscription you've already bought with the same Apple ID.

What's free, and what needs Recovery Pro?

Most of the app is free — finding meetings, browsing events, tracking your clean time, the community feed, connecting with members, and Noah's check-ins. Recovery Pro unlocks unlimited journaling and step work, your full Sponsorship Tree, direct messaging, and more.

How do I report a post?

Tap the ··· menu on any community post and choose Report post. We review every report and act within 24 hours.

How do I block someone?

Tap the ··· menu on a post and choose Block. Their posts disappear from your feed immediately. You can manage blocked members from your account profile.

How do I delete my account?

Open your profile, go to the Account section, and choose Delete Account. This permanently removes your profile, journal entries, connections, and all associated data. It cannot be undone.

Is Clean Living connected to NA?

No. Clean Living is an independent recovery tool and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Narcotics Anonymous or NA World Services.

If you're struggling right now

Clean Living is a community tool, not a crisis service. If you are in crisis or thinking about harming yourself, please reach out to your local emergency services or a helpline straight away. You don't have to do this alone.
